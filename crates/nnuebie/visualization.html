NNUE Feature Indexing

Visualize why make_index exists at all.

This page shows make_index as an address builder, not as mysterious chess magic. The whole job is: turn one board fact into one feature id so the engine can look up the right first-stage weight vector and the right 8 PSQT values.

Inputs

Change the inputs and watch how the feature id is built from three parts: king-bucket block, piece band, and one square slot inside that band.

Live Formula

Why king buckets?

King location matters to evaluation, but using all 64 exact king squares would make the feature space much bigger. So the file groups similar king positions into 32 blocks and stores one top-level feature block per bucket.

Why piece offsets?

After choosing the king-bucket block, the engine still has to separate “white pawn on e4” from “black queen on e4”. Piece offsets just choose which 64-square band inside that king-bucket block belongs to that piece group.

Why 64 slots?

Once you know the king bucket and the piece group, you still need one slot for every possible square on the board. That is why each piece band is 64 slots wide.
